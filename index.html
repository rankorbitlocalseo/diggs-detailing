What Mistakes Should You Avoid When Choosing a Car Detailing Service?

📍 Chesapeake, VA
7 min read
Diggs Mobile Auto Care
← Back to Home
Your car is one of the biggest investments you'll make. So when it's time to get it detailed, you want someone who will treat it right. Finding a trustworthy professional car detailing service isn't always as easy as it sounds. There are plenty of options out there, but not all of them deliver what they promise. Some cut corners. Some charge hidden fees. And some just don't care about quality. Knowing what mistakes to avoid can save you from wasting money and ending up with a car that looks barely better than when you dropped it off.

A good detail makes your car feel brand new again. A bad one leaves you frustrated and still looking at swirl marks, dirty carpets, or missed spots. I've talked to dozens of car owners who wished they'd asked more questions before handing over their keys. Let's walk through the most common mistakes so you can skip the headache and find a detailer who actually delivers.

Mistake #1: Booking based on price alone

We all love a good deal. But when it comes to detailing, rock bottom prices usually mean rock bottom quality. A detailer charging fifty bucks for a full interior and exterior is probably using harsh chemicals, skipping important steps, or rushing through the job. Real detailing takes time. A proper full detail can take three or four hours. If the price seems too good to be true, it almost always is.

🔍 What a real detail should include

  • Thorough interior vacuum and carpet extraction
  • Hand wash, not just a spray and wipe
  • Upholstery and leather cleaning and conditioning
  • Attention to door jambs, vents, and tight spaces

Mistake #2: Ignoring reviews and reputation

A detailer can promise the world, but past customers will tell you the truth. Skip the five star reviews that just say "great job" without details. Look for reviews that mention specific things like attention to detail, punctuality, and whether the detailer actually cleaned the areas they said they would. A consistent pattern of happy customers is worth more than any advertisement.

Mistake #3: Not asking about products and equipment

What a detailer uses matters. Cheap, harsh chemicals can damage your paint, dry out your leather, or leave residue everywhere. A professional uses quality products designed for automotive surfaces. They should also have proper equipment like steam cleaners, extractors, and soft microfiber towels. Don't be shy about asking what they use and why.

I've seen cars come out of cheap detail shops with hazy windows, greasy dashboards, and water spots baked into the paint. The detailer used dish soap and a dirty rag. That's not detailing. That's just a wet car. Good work requires good tools and good products.

Mistake #4: Choosing a shop that doesn't come to you

Mobile detailing is a game changer. A service that comes to your home or office saves you hours of waiting around. You don't have to drop your car off, find a ride, and then come back later. You just park, hand over the keys, and go about your day. Mobile detailers also bring their own water and power, so they don't need anything from you.

Mistake #5: Not checking what's included in the price

Some detailers advertise a low price, then charge extra for everything else. Want the wheels cleaned? Extra. Want the door jambs wiped? Extra. Pet hair removal? Definitely extra. Before you book, ask for a clear list of what's included in the package. A transparent detailer will tell you up front. A shady one will surprise you at the end.

Questions to ask before booking

✓ What's included in your interior detail?
✓ Do you use steam cleaning or just spray and wipe?
✓ What brand of products do you use?
✓ Are there any hidden fees or surcharges?
✓ How long will the service take?
✓ Do you offer any warranty or satisfaction guarantee?

Mistake #6: Forgetting about add-ons and extras

Sometimes the basic package isn't enough. If your car has heavy stains, pet hair everywhere, or foggy headlights, you might need extra services. A good detailer will offer add-ons like odor elimination, leather conditioning, headlight restoration, and engine bay cleaning. Ask about these upfront so you're not disappointed when the basic detail doesn't fix everything.

Mistake #7: Waiting too long between details

A one time detail is great, but regular maintenance is what keeps your car looking good. Some people wait until their car is trashed before calling a detailer. That makes the job harder and more expensive. A maintenance detail every couple of months preserves your paint, protects your interior, and actually costs less in the long run.

Mistake #8: Not checking if they're insured and professional

Anyone can call themselves a detailer. But professionals carry insurance. That matters. If something gets damaged accidentally, you want to know you're covered. Also look for signs of professionalism like clear communication, arriving on time, and having a system for booking and payment.

Mistake #9: Overlooking the importance of communication

Does the detailer answer your questions clearly? Do they listen to your concerns? Do they explain what they'll do and how long it will take? Good communication before the job usually means good communication during and after. If they're hard to reach or vague with answers, that's a red flag.

Mistake #10: Not getting a satisfaction guarantee

A confident detailer stands behind their work. They should offer to make things right if you're not happy. That's not being picky. That's expecting what you paid for. Ask about their guarantee before you book. A simple "we'll fix any missed spots" goes a long way.

Conclusion

Choosing the right car detailing service isn't complicated, but it does take a little homework. Avoid the mistake of shopping by price alone. Read real reviews from real customers. Ask about products, equipment, and what's included. And don't underestimate the value of a mobile service that comes to you.
